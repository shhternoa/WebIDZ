Popular Places

Natural History Museum

Even if you are not interested in natural history, it is definitely worth going to South Kensington and looking at the museum building. And if you decide to go inside, you will be very surprised by the vastness of the exposition and the number of exhibitions.

Cromwell Rd, South Kensington, London SW7 5BD

Covent Garden

Central London, where life is in full swing around the clock. Do you want to feel the pulse of life here? This means that you are here, where many shops, restaurants, pubs and cafes are concentrated.

7 Bow Street, London, WC2E 9DD, England

Buckingham Palace

Like Westminster Palace, the residence of the Queen of Great Britain is open to the public, but many people prefer to look at it from behind the gate. If you still want to know how monarchs live, you can join the excursion group.

London SW1A 1AA, UK
